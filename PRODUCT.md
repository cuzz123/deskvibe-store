# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Remote workers, creators, and desk-conscious professionals. They shop during work hours — comparing materials, reviews, and design signals — and want confidence they're buying something lasting, not disposable office gear. They care about how their workspace looks and feels. Price-sensitive enough to appreciate honest value, but willing to pay for quality that lasts.

## Product Purpose

Curate premium desk accessories that make workspaces as beautiful as they are functional. DeskVibe exists because the market is split between ugly plastic office gear and overpriced designer pieces — there's a middle ground of premium materials, honest prices, and designs that actually look good on your desk. Success means repeat buyers who trust the brand for their entire desk ecosystem, from monitor stands to cable management to lighting.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, Material-honest, Curated.** Confident without luxury pretension. The voice says "we chose these materials because they're the right ones," not "this is expensive."

Voice dimensions (all four are active):
- **Expert confidence** — trust through transparency about materials and process, not claims
- **Curator's eye** — every product is chosen, not stocked; descriptions reward curiosity
- **Honest premium** — price is justified by craft, never defensive. "Aerospace-grade aluminum because it's the right thing, not because it sounds expensive"
- **Restrained warmth** — Scandinavian directness. Say less, show more. Let product photography and materials speak

## Anti-references

1. **Marketplace density** (Amazon, Walmart) — crowded grids, aggressive discount badges, competing CTAs, decision fatigue. DeskVibe is a studio, not a warehouse.
2. **Cold SaaS aesthetic** — sterile blue-white-grey palettes, abstract illustrations, generic gradient backdrops, tech-startup "modern." No tactile warmth.
3. **Luxury-fashion pretension** — black backgrounds, dramatic serif typography, aspirational-but-impractical presentation. This is beautiful gear for real desks, not a runway.

## Design Principles

1. **Material speaks first.** Let product photography, material descriptions, and tactile cues lead. Every visual choice should reinforce "these are real, premium materials." Wood grain, metal finish, textile texture — show them.

2. **Show, don't sell.** Scandinavian directness applied to e-commerce. Trust through transparency (materials, process, reviews), not hype. No manufactured urgency, no inflated claims. If the product is good, the details prove it.

3. **Curate, don't catalog.** Amazon shows everything; DeskVibe shows the right things. Intentionality in product placement, collection groupings, and recommendations. A product missing from a page is a choice, not an oversight.

4. **Warmth through substance.** Warmth comes from natural materials, real photography, and truthful copy — not from cream backgrounds, soft gradients, or sentimental language. Let wood, wool, and linen carry the warmth; let the interface stay clean and confident.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target:** WCAG 2.1 AA
- **Key considerations:** Sufficient color contrast (≥4.5:1 for body text, ≥3:1 for large text), keyboard-navigable cart and checkout, screen-reader-friendly product pages with proper ARIA, focus indicators on all interactive elements, reduced-motion alternatives for carousel and hover animations
